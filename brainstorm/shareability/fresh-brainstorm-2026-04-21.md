# Shareability Brainstorm — Fresh, 2026-04-21

**Purpose:** Blow the idea space wide open before consolidating. Not a roadmap. Not a ranking. A menu — so we can look at it together and ask "which of these actually matter?"

**Reset:** Ignore the prior Strategy-Card-first framing. Start from the question: *in an investment platform, what can be made shareable?*

---

## What changed since the last brainstorm (5 days ago)

These matter because they compress our window and reshape the competitive ground.

- **Robinhood Social is no longer a 1k-user beta.** As of March 2026 it's a general-rollout product — an X-style microblogging feed where every post must be tied to a verified trade, with follow + copy-trading built in. The "6–12 month window" I wrote in the session brief is probably closer to 3–6 months now.
- **Blossom (now 500k+ members globally)** shipped portfolio milestones, dividend summaries, and a redesigned trade widget in 2026. That's the "Moments" direction — they got there first.
- **Gen Z is already there.** 55% of Gen Z investors say social media is how they got into investing; 37% actively seek financial advice on TikTok/IG. The behavior we're trying to enable already exists in the audience — what's missing is the *broker-native tool* for it.
- **Investing creator economy is real.** eToro's Popular Investor program pays up to 2% of AUM to people who get copied. Vivian Tu (Your Rich BFF): 2.7M TikTok, 3.8M IG. There's real money in being a "picked by" investor.
- **"Wrapped" is now a category.** Spotify, Strava, Duolingo all do it. The format is so well-understood that every category of app is expected to have one.
- **There's an existing meme layer.** "Portfolio review" is already a TikTok genre — "POV: bro gives you a stock pick and your portfolio starts looking like this." The cultural language of sharing portfolios already exists; we just need to hand users a better tool than a screenshot.

## The brainstorm

I'm going to go wide across four dimensions. Don't try to rank these as you read — just skim for things that spark.

1. **WHAT** to share — the object (biggest bucket)
2. **HOW** to share — the format/channel
3. **MECHANICS** — what makes sharing actually happen
4. **RECIPIENT SIDE** — what makes the loop close

---

## 1. WHAT to share — the shareable object

### 1A. Self / identity

1. **Investing style card** — auto-generated archetype ("Long-Horizon Trend Follower," "Contrarian Accumulator," "Thematic Explorer") from actual behavior. Wrapped-style.
2. **Investing personality quiz** — BuzzFeed-style 8-question quiz, result is a shareable card. Onboarding for non-users, identity-affirmer for users.
3. **"My investing origin story"** — first trade, first strategy, how long I've been investing, ordered as a 9:16 narrative scroll.
4. **Risk profile card** — "I'm a 7/10 on risk." Visual sliders, no numbers, no dollars.
5. **Investing zodiac** — pick an archetype mascot (the Builder, the Explorer, the Collector, the Skeptic). Gamified identity.
6. **"Since I joined Surmount…"** — a day-one vs today card, anniversary-framed, percent only.

### 1B. Holdings / portfolio

7. **Allocation donut card** — sector/style breakdown, privacy ladder (percent-only / sector-only / top-5-only) chosen at share time.
8. **Top 5 holdings card** — logos + symbols, no weights by default (weights optional).
9. **Portfolio X-ray** — "my portfolio is 62% growth, 28% income, 10% speculation." Categorical, taste-signaling.
10. **"My top conviction"** — single pick Maya bets biggest on, with her own one-line thesis.
11. **"The boring part of my portfolio"** — anti-flex card that shows the steady-eddie ETFs or dividend stocks. Self-deprecating belonging.
12. **Diversification card** — visual representation of how spread the portfolio is (5 sectors, 14 holdings, etc.).
13. **"My portfolio vs. the market"** — non-dollar comparison card showing relative composition. ("I'm 40% more tech-weighted than the S&P.") Compliance-sensitive — percentages of composition, not performance.

### 1C. Strategies / picks

14. **Strategy card** *(current build)* — marketplace or no-code-built strategy, shareable with Maya's take.
15. **"I made this"** — a no-code-built strategy with the build date, rule count, backtest chart (normalized). Emphasizes craft.
16. **Strategy remix card** — "I took [Maya's strategy] and tweaked it" → attribution chain.
17. **"I'm in this with [X] friends"** — social proof on a strategy card.
18. **Strategy changelog** — "I updated my 'tech-heavy' strategy today, swapping AAPL for AMD." Share-your-thinking card.
19. **Strategy comparison card** — "here are three strategies I'm choosing between." Invites others into the decision.

### 1D. Watchlists / interests

20. **Watchlist card** — 5–8 tickers Maya is tracking, no positions, no dollars. One-line stance.
21. **Watchlist diff card** — "What I added to my watchlist this week." Weekly drumbeat.
22. **"Stocks I almost bought"** — confessional format. Trades she *didn't* pull the trigger on. Relatable.
23. **Theme watchlist** — "My AI hardware watchlist" — a themed bundle Maya curated.
24. **Heatmap card** — Maya's watchlist as a sector/industry heatmap. Visual-first.

### 1E. Moments / milestones (behavioral, not performance)

25. **1-year Surmount anniversary** — auto-card on the day.
26. **First trade** — "Maya made her first trade on Surmount today." Frame-worthy.
27. **100th trade / 1,000th trade** — cumulative behavioral milestones.
28. **First dividend** — dividend payment notification → "I got paid by AAPL today" card.
29. **First negative year held through** — survivor-signaling without specifying the P&L.
30. **"Held through the April dip"** — event-anchored callout.
31. **Diversification milestone** — "Added my 5th sector."
32. **Wrapped (quarterly / yearly)** — the big annual moment. Surmount Wrapped.

### 1F. Performance / outcomes (compliance-heavy — most of this needs legal)

33. **"I beat the S&P last quarter"** — benchmark comparison, percentage-based. Red compliance.
34. **Contrarian-correct card** — "I bought NVDA when 74% of Surmount users sold." Red.
35. **Dividend income cadence** — "Every 18 days a new dividend." No dollars, just cadence. Yellow.
36. **Streak card** — "52 weeks of investing," "held TSLA through 6 corrections." Green.
37. **Percentile card** — "You're in the top 20% of Surmount users by sector diversity." Requires legal.
38. **Best-decision card** — "My best-performing strategy this year was X." Percentage only, anonymized baseline.

### 1G. Process / craft

39. **Backtest time-lapse video** — 5–10 second video of the builder working. TikTok/Reels-native.
40. **"Show the work" card** — Maya's step-by-step strategy thesis (3 slides, tap-through).
41. **Before/after portfolio card** — "Here's how I rebalanced this quarter." Composition-only.
42. **Rule-set card** — "My buy rules for dividend stocks." Visual rule list, templatable.
43. **Builder replay** — others can scrub through *how* Maya built a strategy, step by step.

### 1H. Thoughts / commentary

44. **Conviction card** — single ticker + tweet-length thesis. Editorial, opinion-first.
45. **Thesis essay** — long-form post hosted on Surmount under `/thesis/username/slug`. Substack-in-the-broker.
46. **Quick take card** — 60-character reaction to a news event, tied to a ticker.
47. **Call card** — "I'm calling it: AMD before NVDA by Q3." Public prediction, tracked. Needs legal.
48. **AMA card** — "I'm taking questions about my strategy for 2 hours." Live-moment social.

### 1I. Relationships / social proof

49. **"Following Maya"** — recipient-side card celebrating Alex's 6 months following Maya.
50. **Friend-in-this-strategy badge** — "Priya is also in this." Opt-in only.
51. **Portfolio co-op card** — Maya and a friend building a strategy together. Collab object.
52. **Investing circle card** — group of 4–6 friends, named ("The Tuesday Investors"), who share strategies internally.
53. **Credit/attribution card** — "5 friends joined because of Maya" → Maya's recognition card.

### 1J. Learning / education

54. **Lesson completed card** — "I learned how options work today." Duolingo-style knowledge cards.
55. **"Glossary of the week"** — Maya's pick of a new concept she learned.
56. **Book/podcast recommendation card** — investing content Maya recommends.
57. **Mistake card** — "What I got wrong in 2025." Vulnerability-as-taste.

### 1K. Goals / lifestyle

58. **Goal progress card** — "75% to my house fund." Named goals, percentages only.
59. **"What I'm investing for"** — goal declaration, no numbers.
60. **Life-stage card** — "Invested through buying my first apartment." Ties investing to life milestones.

### 1L. Market reactions / news takes

61. **News-reaction card** — "My reaction to today's CPI print: I'm holding." Ticker tie-in.
62. **Earnings card** — Maya's take on a holding's earnings. Percentage-composition mentioned, no dollars.
63. **Market-day card** — "How my portfolio moved today." Percent-only.
64. **"What I'd buy with $1,000 right now"** — speculative, hypothetical. Engagement-bait but tasteful.

### 1M. Challenges / games

65. **Pick a stock for the year** — annual challenge card, tracked publicly, shared at end-of-year.
66. **Paper-trading tournament** — group challenge, weekly leaderboard card.
67. **Streak challenge** — "30 days of investing $10/day." Shareable progress.
68. **"Blind pick"** — pick a stock without seeing its ticker for 30 days, reveal at the end. Game-y.
69. **Guess-the-stock card** — Maya posts anonymized chart/metrics, friends guess. TikTok-style.

## 3. MECHANICS — what actually makes sharing happen

### 3A. Making the share feel good
1. **"Your take"** caption layer — 60 chars of Maya's voice on every card.
2. **Privacy ladder at share time** — choose what you show per-share, not in settings.
3. **Tasteful defaults** — even if Maya skips the caption, the card doesn't look generic.
4. **Preview-before-post** — full fidelity preview so there are no surprises.
5. **Remix templates** — start from a popular template, personalize.
6. **Background swap** — pick from a curated set of backgrounds; no photo upload (brand-safety).
7. **Multi-card sequence** — string 3–5 cards into an IG Story sequence, pre-ordered.

### 3B. Making sharing repeatable
8. **Weekly / monthly drumbeat** — "Your week in investing is ready." Repeated reason to share.
9. **Auto-triggered moments** — card generates itself, Maya chooses to share or dismiss.
10. **"One more?"** suggestion — after a share, suggest a related shareable. Chain sessions.
11. **Saved drafts** — Maya can draft cards now and share later (for IG-posting windows).
12. **Scheduled posting** — connect to Buffer/Later, post at optimal time.

### 3C. Social mechanics
13. **Attribution chain** — when Priya shares Maya's strategy, Maya gets credit visibly.
14. **Share → unlock** — unlock a feature by sharing (gamified but can feel gross; use sparingly).
15. **Invite-gate** — some features require inviting 1 friend. Classic but abrasive.
16. **Referral rewards** — cash, free strategies, premium trial for successful installs.
17. **Streaks & challenges** — invite friends to share/invest together.
18. **Collab cards** — two users build one card together (hand-off flow).
19. **Group strategies** — a small circle invests together in a strategy.
20. **Creator profiles** — Maya has a public profile; strategies she's shared live there.
21. **Creator monetization** — copy-trading revenue, tip jar, paid strategy subscription.
22. **Reputation system** — badges, ranks, verified picks.

### 3D. The "not-brag" layer
23. **Auto-suppression rules** — down 20%? don't trigger a card.
24. **"Dignified loss" cards** — explicit loss-framing done tastefully ("I held through a rough quarter").
25. **App-as-narrator** — "Surmount noticed..." framing takes the bragging off Maya.
26. **Opt-in visibility** — every field on every card can be toggled per share.
27. **Compliance copy generator** — auto-appends "not investment advice" where needed.

## 4. RECIPIENT SIDE — what closes the loop

1. **Deep link to exact strategy/ticker** — not the home screen.
2. **Maya's take persists on the landing page** — recipient sees the human context.
3. **Web preview without install** — readable page if no app installed.
4. **Guest mode** — browse without signing up; prompt to install on invest action.
5. **Deferred deep link** — post-install, land exactly where the card sent them.
6. **One-tap action** — Invest, Follow, Add to Watchlist, Copy, Save.
7. **"Because Maya shared this"** banner on the landing page.
8. **Tap-to-remix** — recipient takes Maya's strategy and tweaks it in the builder.
9. **Counter-offer** — "Maya shared this. Here's what I'd do differently." (response card)
10. **Comment back to sharer** — in-app reply to the share, no need to switch to iMessage.
11. **Save-for-later** — recipient can bookmark Maya's share without committing.
12. **Recipient analytics** — Maya sees "4 friends saw your card, 1 invested."

## Cross-cutting themes worth flagging

**(a) The identity layer is where the defensible moat lives.**
Anyone can build a share-to-IG-Story button. What's hard to copy is *who you are as an investor*: your archetype, your streak of picks, your following, your attribution chain. That stack is the creator layer. Robinhood Social is building this on top of verified trades; we can build it on top of strategies + taste. Strategy-as-identity is a cleaner primitive than trades-as-identity.

**(b) Every share format has a "taste-vs-brag" axis.**
Portfolio P&L = brag. Watchlist = taste. Performance = brag. Thesis = taste. Streak = ambiguous. Design decisions across all features should be driven by "which axis are we pushing?" — the brag axis is where compliance and cringe both live.

**(c) The real question is which shareable object is the *primitive* that other shares attach to.**
Strategy? Holding? User identity? Moment? All of the above works, but one of them is the atom. Spotify's atom is the track. Strava's atom is the activity. TikTok's atom is the 15-second clip. Surmount's atom could be the strategy, the holding, or the user herself. That choice shapes everything downstream.

**(d) Creator monetization is probably adjacent-to-inevitable.**
If a slice of Surmount users become sources of picks for others (which the Robinhood/eToro playbook confirms will happen), we need a story for compensating them. Ignoring it surrenders the network effect to Robinhood Social. Addressing it means thinking about paid strategies, tip jars, or AUM-based revenue from day 1 of the share system design.

**(e) We are closer to competitive closure than my previous note said.**
Robinhood Social is now in broad rollout. Blossom shipped the Moments layer. The unoccupied space is narrower than it was a month ago — the distinctive one is still *strategy-first + recipient-actionable + not inside a walled garden*. But the window is probably 3–6 months, not 6–12. Any brainstorm should hold this in mind.

## How I'd structure consolidation

When you're ready to collapse this to a v1 menu, three questions:

1. **What's the atom?** Is the shareable unit the strategy, the holding, the user, or the moment? Pick one as the primary primitive; others attach to it.
2. **Which 3 types of sharing serve the Maya audience most, compliance-respectingly?** From this list, pick 3 that (a) have high taste-not-brag fit, (b) a recipient action, (c) Green or Yellow on compliance.
3. **Which meta-feature is non-negotiable?** Of all the mechanics in §3, which 2–3 are the ones we *must* ship regardless of which cards we pick — the privacy ladder, the attribution chain, the take layer, the deferred deep link?

We can pair-work on those three questions in the next pass.
